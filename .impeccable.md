# My Homeschool Curriculum — Design Context

Last updated: 2026-04-22

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary user:** A parent (typically mom) who is new to homeschooling. She's
researching for the upcoming school year, has kids in early-to-mid elementary,
and is making a consequential financial and educational decision for her family
for the first time. She arrives feeling overwhelmed by the sheer number of
options scattered across Facebook groups, YouTube reviews, and catalog PDFs.

**Context of use:** Often at the kitchen table after kids go to bed, on a laptop
or tablet. Sometimes on her phone during nap time. She may have 15 minutes at a
time between interruptions — not a long uninterrupted research session.

**Job to be done:** Narrow 60+ options down to a confident shortlist of 2–3 that
fit her family's grade level, teaching style, worldview, and budget — without
spending another weekend on it.

### Brand Personality

**Three words (from founder):** Trustworthy · Helpful · _[third word still open — defaulting to_ **grounded** _— flag this]_

**Voice:** Personal, no-BS helpful, like a friend who already did the research
and is handing you her notes. Not corporate, not salesy, not listicle-cute.
Matches Vanessa's founder voice: military veteran + MBA + mom who "just wants
something that works without hours of extra planning."

**Emotional goals — what the user feels when leaving a session:**

1. **Confident** — "I can make this decision. I know what to look for."
2. **Validated** — "My priorities (budget, worldview, style) are respected here.
   This site is not pushing me toward any one answer."

**What the brand explicitly is NOT:**

- Not an MLM landing page. No hype headlines, no testimonial carousels dripping
  with "TRANSFORMED MY LIFE."
- Not a BuzzFeed listicle. Not "17 Homeschool Curricula You Won't Believe…"
- Not an edtech SaaS demo. No neon gradients, no abstract geometric hero
  illustrations, no "powered by AI" badges.
- Not corporate sterile. This is a small, personal operation — it should feel
  hand-built, not VC-polished.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Palette (committed — formal brand system in place):**

- Navy `#1F3A4D` — primary surface, headings, trust
- Gold `#D4A84C` — accent, italic emphasis, CTAs on light surfaces
- Coral `#D57A5A` — secondary accent, CTAs on navy
- Sage `#7A9E7E` / Deep Sage `#4A7550` — tertiary (success, filters, tags)
- Cream `#FDF6EC` / Warm White `#FFFBF5` — page backgrounds
- Paper `#F0EBE5` — subtle surface variation

Neutrals are already tinted warm (cream/paper base) — consistent with the brand
hue. Keep this.

**Typography (committed):**

- Fraunces (display + wordmark, italic "Curriculum" is the signature move)
- DM Sans (body + UI)
- Playfair Display (legacy fallback; Fraunces is primary)

**Theme:** Light-only currently. Dark mode was NOT requested. _Flag for later:
late-night browsing is a real use case — worth revisiting if bounce rates are
high in that window._

**Tone landing:** Warm editorial — Fraunces serif headings with italic gold
accents, generous spacing, real photography over illustration, asymmetric layouts
avoided in favor of readable columns. Think closer to NYT Wirecutter / Cup of Jo
than Notion or Linear.

### Design Principles

These guide all future design decisions on this project:

1. **Trust over delight.** A new homeschool parent needs to believe the site is
   honest and independent before anything else. Warm, confident, clean beats
   playful or dazzling every time. No decorative flourishes that don't serve
   the information.

2. **Editorial, not dashboard.** This is a research hub, not a SaaS app. Lean
   into magazine-style typography, real photography, long-form readability,
   and content that looks like it was written by a human who cares. Avoid
   metric tiles, gradient borders, and glassmorphism.

3. **Founder voice is always visible.** Vanessa's face and story are the single
   strongest trust signal the product has. Design decisions should never
   compete with or abstract away the personal-human-built feel.

4. **Respect the decision weight.** A curriculum purchase is hundreds of dollars
   and a year of a child's education. Every comparison, filter, and
   recommendation should acknowledge that weight — no trivializing animations,
   no "fun" error states, no flippant copy.

5. **Accessibility is table stakes.** WCAG AA minimum (already implemented).
   Special-needs filters on the product (dyslexia, ADHD, autism) imply readers
   may include parents of kids with learning differences — body copy should
   favor clear sans (DM Sans) over decorative serifs at small sizes, and
   reduced-motion preferences must be respected (already honored on parallax
   + scroll animations).
